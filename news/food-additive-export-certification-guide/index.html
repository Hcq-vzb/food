Food Additive Export Certification Guide

Published 2026-06-14 · Food Ingredients China

Understanding food additive export certification requirements helps plant managers and buyers make confident equipment decisions. This guide summarizes practical criteria aligned with current industry practice.

Documentation Buyers Request

Importers expect CoA per lot, MSDS, allergen statements, and country-specific compliance letters. Organize digital dossiers before trade show or audit visits.

ISO and FSSC for Ingredient Plants

ISO 22000 and FSSC 22000 demonstrate HACCP-based food safety. Many EU and North American buyers treat third-party certification as a baseline qualification.

Regional Registration Rules

EU food additive categories, US GRAS or FDA notification paths, and Middle East Halal certificates each add lead time — plan 3–6 months for new SKU export.

Labeling and Traceability

Correct E-numbers, INS codes, and maximum usage levels must appear on spec sheets. Batch coding and recall drills prove traceability to retail clients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all thickeners need individual EU approval?

Export status depends on additive category and Annex listings — verify with your regulatory consultant.

How often renew Halal certificates?

Typically annually; align production audits with certificate validity.

Can one CoA cover multiple shipments?

Each production lot requires its own CoA tied to batch number.
